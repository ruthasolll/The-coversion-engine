# Discovery Call Context Brief — Template

This is the internal briefing document the agent attaches to every Cal.com calendar invite for a booked discovery call. The human delivery lead (typically Arun or Yabebal) reads this brief 10 minutes before the call and runs the call with the context already loaded.

**The quality of this brief is the single biggest lever the agent has on discovery-to-proposal conversion.** A shallow context brief leads to a call where the human spends the first 15 minutes re-establishing what the agent already qualified. A sharp context brief lets the human land on the specific failure mode or opportunity within the first 5 minutes.

---

## How sections get filled

Each template variable resolves from a specific source:

| Variable prefix | Source |
|---|---|
| `prospect_*`, `call_*`, `thread_*` | HubSpot contact + conversation thread |
| `segment_*`, `funding_*`, `hiring_*`, `ai_maturity_*` | `hiring_signal_brief.json` |
| `gap_*`, `peer_*` | `competitor_gap_brief.json` |
| `bench_*`, `availability_*` | `bench_summary.json` |
| `prospect_insight_*`, `objection_*`, `urgency_*` | Langfuse trace of the thread |
| `suggested_*` | LLM call with style guide + transcripts as context |
| `confident_*`, `uncertain_*`, `missing_*` | Agent's self-reflection call |

## What a good brief looks like

- Every section is filled, not skipped.
- The agent names what it does not know in Section 10 rather than pretending completeness.
- Section 6 surfaces objections the delivery lead will not have caught from the thread alone — the value-add is the synthesis.
- Section 9 has at least one entry. Every prospect thread has at least one thing the delivery lead should avoid doing.
- The brief is **at most one scroll on a laptop screen**. Longer briefs are skipped by humans; that defeats the purpose.

## What a bad brief looks like

- Empty Section 7 (commercial signals) because the agent did not look.
- Generic Section 8 ("do a standard discovery call") — wastes the delivery lead's pre-call time.
- Section 3 citing a competitor gap that turns out to be factually wrong — single largest brand-reputation risk. The delivery lead will discover this in the call and lose trust in all agent briefs going forward.
- Section 10 marked "high confidence on everything" — implausible, signals the agent is not self-aware.

## Grading note

The brief's quality is measured in Act IV's ablation. A brief that leads to a proposal request has higher value than one that doesn't, but the causal link is noisy — a prospect may have been ready regardless. The cleaner measure is the delivery lead's post-call rating of the brief: "did this brief save me time and land me in the right conversation?" Program staff will collect this rating during the pilot if the system is selected for real deployment.
