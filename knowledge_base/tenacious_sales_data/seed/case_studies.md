## Case Study 1 — Global Advertising Technology Platform

**Client descriptor:** Public AdTech platform. ~500 employees. Processes 30 billion daily transactions. Serves major media outlets (including top-tier UK and US broadcasters).

**Engagement type:** Managed talent outsourcing (primary) + fixed-scope ML system build (secondary).

### Situation

The platform had an in-house data-science function that was stretched across too many parallel initiatives. The VP Engineering wanted to scale machine-learning output — specifically bidding-system optimization — without the 4–6 month hiring cycle for senior ML engineers in London and New York.

### What Tenacious delivered

- Set up and scaled a dedicated offshore data-science team inside the client's delivery structure.
- Built the data-lake pipelines feeding the bidding system from multiple log streams.
- Developed the A/B testing framework used for hypothesis validation across creative and audience targeting.
- Delivered real-time bidding ML models and customer-targeting / KPI-optimization algorithms.

### Outcomes

- **X% margin improvement** on the primary revenue line the bidding system serves.
- **~X person-hours of effort saved per year** on manual ad-operations work previously handled by the London team.
- **Engagement duration:** ongoing, currently in year 2.
- **Team size:** 5 data scientists, and 5 data engineers.


### Why this case converts prospects

The X% margin number is credible and specific. The X person-hours is a human-readable headline. The agent may cite both figures, but must not extrapolate them to other clients or claim an equivalent outcome is guaranteed.

**Quotable language for the agent:**
> "One of our long-running engagements — a global AdTech platform processing 30 billion daily transactions — saw an X% margin improvement on the bidding line where we embedded a dedicated ML team."

## Case Study 2 — North American Loyalty Platform

**Client descriptor:** Major North American company loyalty and rewards program. ~X employees in the parent company; loyalty-program unit is ~X people.

**Engagement type:** Project consulting (fixed scope, multi-phase).

### Situation

The loyalty program onboarded new corporate partners through a manual 17-step configuration process that took their partnerships team several months per partner. The VP Loyalty wanted to collapse this to days using an AI-driven configuration assistant — not a rewrite of the platform, but a layer on top that could reason about the configuration rules and propose structured setup packages for review.

### What Tenacious delivered

- An AI-driven configuration assistant integrated into the existing partnerships tooling.
- Validation rules checking configuration consistency (e.g., currency conversion logic, MSRP vs wholesale pricing defaults) before pushing to production.
- Documentation and training for the partnerships team on reviewing the assistant's proposals.

### Outcomes

- **Onboarding time reduced** from several months to a few days per new partner.
- **Qualitative outcome:** the partnerships team shifted from manual configuration to a review-and-approve workflow, freeing senior staff for negotiation and relationship work.
- **Engagement duration:** 3 phases over ~6 months. Currently in Phase 1.

### Why this case converts prospects

The "months to days" language is vivid and specific without over-claiming. It works particularly well for Segment 4 prospects (specialized capability gaps) who are contemplating a similar AI-layer-on-top-of-existing-platform build.

**Quotable language for the agent:**
> "For a North American loyalty program, we built an AI configuration layer that took partner onboarding from months to days — not by rewriting their platform, but by giving the partnerships team an assistant that could reason about the configuration rules."

## Case Study 3 — Multi-Location Fitness Franchise

**Client descriptor:** Multi-location franchise operator in the fitness and wellness sector. ~200 franchise locations across North America. Headquarters team of ~50.

**Engagement type:** Multi-phase project consulting — sales and lead management platform.

### Situation

The franchise operator ran three parallel sales motions — teacher training program admissions, franchise sales to prospective operators, and membership sales at the studio level — each on a different platform (GHL, Twilio, Mariana Tek) with no shared lead pipeline or scoring. The CEO wanted a unified sales-automation layer that could qualify, nurture, and route leads across all three motions without replacing the operational systems the studios already used.

### What Tenacious delivered

- A 5-phase build plan covering: lead ingestion across platforms; unified qualification and scoring; nurture-sequence orchestration; territory intelligence for studio assignment; knowledge retrieval for sales reps in the field.
- Integration layer connecting the three operational platforms without requiring migration.
- A React / NestJS / dbt full-stack codebase delivered in collaboration with the client's technical partner.

### Outcomes

- **Engagement duration:** 5 phases, full engagement running through 2026.
- **Phase 1 deliverable:** completed within the first contracted window; client approved continuation to Phase 2.
- **Specific quantitative outcomes:** to be published after the engagement concludes; the client has a public-reference agreement pending.

### Why this case converts prospects

This case is best used for Segment 4 prospects in multi-location or multi-platform operations who need an integration layer rather than a rebuild. **The agent should not cite specific quantitative outcomes for this engagement** — the numbers are not yet public. Reference the shape of the engagement only.

**Quotable language for the agent:**
> "We're currently mid-engagement with a North American multi-location franchise operator on a 5-phase sales-automation platform — connecting three existing tools without requiring migration. The Phase 1 deliverable cleared in-window; subsequent phases are running on schedule."
