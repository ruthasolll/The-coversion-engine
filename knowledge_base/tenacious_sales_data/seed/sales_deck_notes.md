# Sales Deck — Speaker Notes

Notes for presenters using `seed/sales_deck.pptx` on a discovery call. The
deck is the backdrop, not the script — a good discovery call usually touches
two or three slides, not all ten.

---

## Slide 1 — Cover

Do not read the tagline aloud. Let it sit while you introduce yourself and
ask the prospect how they want the first 10 minutes to go.

---

## Slide 2 — What Tenacious does

The "bridge the gap" framing is deliberate. Most prospects have an AI
ambition and a delivery bottleneck — this slide names the gap without
pretending we close it for everyone. The three service lines are listed in
the order we most commonly sell.

**Common prospect question on this slide:** "Which one fits us?" The right
answer is almost always "we'll figure that out in the next 15 minutes of
this call."

---

## Slide 3 — The unlock

The four stat callouts are grounded in public numbers (`seed/baseline_numbers.md`)
and policy:

- **33% women engineers** — factual, public, updated Jan 2026.
- **100% African engineers** — factual, deliberate positioning, part of the
  public-benefit mission.
- **18 months average tenure** — Tenacious internal, see baseline numbers.
- **7 days typical time-to-deploy** — from `bench_summary.json` for standard
  onboarding. Adds 7 days for regulated clients.

Never round up or restate these numbers differently. If a prospect asks for
more precision (e.g., "what's the 90th-percentile time-to-deploy?"), the
answer is "I don't have that stat to hand — I can send it after the call."
Never improvise a number.

---

## Slide 4 — Who we help

The four segments match `seed/icp_definition.md` exactly. If the prospect
sees themselves in multiple segments, use the classification rules from the
ICP doc (layoff overrides funding; leadership transition wins; etc.) to
route the conversation to the right segment's pitch.

**Common prospect question:** "We're kind of between segments." The right
answer is to pick the dominant signal and talk to that. Do not try to tell
the prospect their segment is hybrid — it makes the whole framework look
soft.

---

## Slide 5 — How we engage

The four-step flow is the default engagement shape. Deviations exist:

- Some prospects want a technical walkthrough before a formal discovery
  call. Skip step 1 and move to step 2.
- Some prospects (Segment 3, transition-window) want to keep the first
  conversation exploratory. Delay step 3 until the second or third call.
- Do **not** skip step 2 for large engagements. The scoping walkthrough is
  where bench-to-brief match gets confirmed; without it, Tenacious risks
  committing to capacity we don't have.

---

## Slide 6 — Pricing

Hardest slide to get right. Two principles:

1. Quote the band, never the total. The band is public; the total requires
   scoping.
2. If a prospect asks "what would a 12-engineer team cost for 18 months?"
   the answer is never a multiplication of the monthly rates. The answer is
   "that specific configuration needs a scoping conversation — the monthly
   rates here are the floor, not the composite price."

The two italicized footnotes on this slide are load-bearing. Never forget
them in the verbal version.

---

## Slide 7 — Case studies

Use one case study per call, not all three. Match the case to the prospect's
segment and situation:

- **Segment 1 / AdTech or B2B platform** → AdTech case study (margin,
  person-hours saved).
- **Segment 3 / new CTO at mid-market** → Airline loyalty case study
  (specific AI layer-on-top, not rebuild).
- **Segment 4 / specialized capability** → Multi-location franchise case
  study (integration without migration).

**Do not** cite specific quantitative outcomes for the franchise case —
those numbers are not yet public. Reference the shape of the engagement
only.

**Never** cite client names aloud on a first call. Named references come on
the scoping walkthrough under NDA with client consent.

---

## Slide 8 — Why us

Six reasons. Hit the two or three that map to the prospect's concerns:

- **"Indian vendor burned us"** → Reliability over hourly rate, Full
  time-zone overlap, Employees not contractors.
- **"Accenture was a slog"** → In-person teams, Employees not contractors.
- **"We don't have much AI expertise in-house"** → AI-native by default.
- **"We care about DEI as well as delivery"** → Public-benefit mission.

Never recite all six. The wall-of-benefit pattern reads as marketing.

---

## Slide 9 — The next 30 minutes

Designed as a closing slide for the discovery call invitation, not the
discovery call itself. If you use this deck during the discovery call,
skip this slide — the call IS the next 30 minutes.

---

## Slide 10 — Contact

Always include both principals. The prospect chooses who to follow up with.

---

## General presentation rules

- **Never read the slide.** The deck is a backdrop. If the prospect looks
  at the deck more than they look at you, the deck is doing too much work.
- **Hide the deck for the second half of the call.** Once scoping
  conversations start, close the deck and share a whiteboard or a shared
  doc.
- **Do not leave the deck open on screen during silence.** If the prospect
  pauses to think, share your face, not a slide.
- **The deck is anonymized for a reason.** Do not use a named-client
  version of this deck in an outbound call unless the client has given
  explicit written consent for that specific use.
